Our client approached us with the vision of a revolutionary consumer smart lock to:

  • View real-time pictures of visitors at your door from your smartphone
  • Review all lock activity recorded and logged by the system
  • Easy installation and replacement of existing lock
  • Provide temporary access for family, guests, contractors etc
  • Allow for proximity-based access via smartphones and programmable fobs

The Solution:

Working on a team of twelve, I took the lead on designing the data architecture and protocol for how data is represented and exchanged throughout the entire system (on embedded devices and on mobile applications).

We used a Ruby on Rails/PostgreSQL/AWS stack for our approach with successful builds that passed through Gerrit and Jenkins being deployed on Heroku. We also handled the cloud aspect of over-the-air (OTA) updates of the firmware for the smart lock.

For this project, we took a test-driven development (TTD) approach, using Rspec in Ruby. This allowed for rapid, clean, and elegant development.

In the end, we developed a RESTful JSON API for the entire IoT system, a secure, extensible cloud system, an integration pipeline, and an admin dashboard.
